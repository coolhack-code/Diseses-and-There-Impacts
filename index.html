Disease and Their Impact

Malaria

what it is:

Malaria is a serious infectious disease causedby plasmodium parasites, spread to humans through the bite of infected female Anopheles mosquitoes.

Common symptoms:

Fever, Chills, Headache, Sweating, Nausea, and Fatigue. Severe cases can cause anemia, Organ failure, orDeath if untreated.

Where it's Common:

Mostly in tropical and subtropical regions, especially Africa, South Asia, and parts of Southeast Asia.

Impact:

=> Causes hundreds of thousands of daeths each year, mainly among children under 5 and pregnanat woman.

=> Leads to lost school and work days, increasing poverty and slowing economic growth.

=> Strains healthcare systems in affected countries

Prevention & Treatment:

Using mosquito nets, repellents, indoor spraying, and prompt treatment with antimalarial medicines can prevent and control the disease.

Diabetes

What it is:

Diabetes is a chronic condition where the body cannot properly control blood sugar (glucose) levels due to problems with insulin (a hormone made by the pancreas).

Main Types:

=> Type 1: The body makes little or no insulin (usually starts in childhood).

=> Type 2: The body doesn’t use insulin properly (most common; linked to lifestyle and genetics).

=> Gestational diabetes: Occurs during pregnancy.

Commonj Symptom:

Frequent urination, excessive thirst, hunger, fatigue, blurred vision, and slow-healing wounds.

Impact:

=> Can damage the heart, kidneys, eyes, and nerves if not controlled.

=> Increases risk of heart disease and stroke.

=> Affects daily life and requires long-term care.

Management:

Healthy diet, regular exercise, monitoring blood sugar, and medicines or insulin help control diabetes.

Cancer:

What it is:

Cancer is a disease in which abnormal cells grow uncontrollably and can spread to other parts of the body.

Causes:

Genetic changes, tobacco use, unhealthy diet, infections, radiation, and exposure to harmful chemicals.

Common Types:

Lung, breast, blood (leukemia), skin, and colon cancer.

Symptoms

Unusual lumps, long-lasting pain, unexplained weight loss, fatigue, changes in skin or bleeding.

Impact:

=> One of the leading causes of death worldwide.

=> Affects physical health, mental well-being, and family life.

=> Treatment can be long and costly, impacting education and work.

Prevention & treatment:

Healthy lifestyle, avoiding tobacco, early screening, and treatments like surgery, chemotherapy, and radiation help control cancer.

Brain Tumour

What it is:

A brain tumour is a mass of abnormal cells growing in or around the brain. It can be benign (non-cancerous) or malignant (cancerous).

Causes:

Often unknown; may involve genetic changes, radiation exposure, or family history.

Common Symptoms:

Headaches, seizures, vomiting, blurred vision, difficulty in speech or movement, and memory problems.

Impact:

=> Affects vital brain functions like movement, thinking, and senses.

=> Can disrupt daily life, school, and work.

=> Severe cases can be life-threatening if untreated.

Treatment:

Depends on type and size—surgery, radiation therapy, and medicines are commonly used.

Heart Attack

What it is:

A heart attack (myocardial infarction) happens when blood flow to part of the heart is blocked, usually by a clot, causing damage to heart muscle.

Main Causes:

Build-up of fatty deposits in arteries (atherosclerosis), smoking, unhealthy diet, lack of exercise, stress, diabetes, and high blood pressure.

Common Symptoms:

Chest pain or pressure, pain in left arm/jaw, shortness of breath, sweating, nausea, and dizziness.

Impact:

=> Can cause permanent heart damage or death if not treated quickly.

=> Affects daily life and may limit physical activity.

=> Major cause of illness and death worldwide.

Prevention & treatment:

Healthy lifestyle, avoiding tobacco, regular exercise, and medicines. Quick medical help greatly improves survival.
